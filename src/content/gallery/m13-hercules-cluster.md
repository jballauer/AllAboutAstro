M13, the Great Globular Cluster in Hercules, is one of the brightest globular clusters visible from the Northern Hemisphere, containing several hundred thousand stars packed into a compact sphere. It lies about 22,200 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 5.8 (just at the edge of naked-eye visibility), and spans roughly 20 arcminutes. Approximate coordinates: RA 16h 41m 41s, Dec +36° 27′ 37″.
