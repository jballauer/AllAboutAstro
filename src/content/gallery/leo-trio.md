The Leo Trio (also called the M66 Group) is a small, gravitationally interacting group of spiral galaxies — M65, M66, and NGC 3628 — in the constellation Leo. All three lie about 35 million light-years away and span roughly 9–10 arcminutes each; M65 shines at magnitude 9.3, M66 at 8.9, and the edge-on NGC 3628 at 9.5. Approximate coordinates: RA 11h 20m, Dec +13°.
