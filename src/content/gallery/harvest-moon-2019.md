The "Harvest Moon" is simply the full moon that falls closest to the autumnal equinox — in 2019, that was September 13/14. At full phase the Moon sits about 384,400 km (238,900 miles) from Earth on average, spans roughly 31 arcminutes across the sky, and shines at an apparent magnitude near -12.7, easily the brightest object in the night sky after the Sun.
