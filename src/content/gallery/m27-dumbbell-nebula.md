M27, the Dumbbell Nebula, was the first planetary nebula ever discovered (by Charles Messier in 1764), in the small constellation Vulpecula. It lies about 1,360 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 7.5, and spans roughly 8 by 5.7 arcminutes, making it one of the brightest planetary nebulae in the sky. Approximate coordinates: RA 19h 59m 36s, Dec +22° 43′ 16″.
