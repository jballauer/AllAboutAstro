The Veil Nebula is the sprawling remnant of a massive star that exploded as a supernova an estimated 10,000–20,000 years ago, in the constellation Cygnus. The full complex — including the western "Witch's Broom" (NGC 6960), the eastern arc (NGC 6992/6995), and central wisps (Pickering's Triangle) — lies about 2,400 light-years away and spans nearly 3 degrees across the sky, roughly six times the width of the full Moon, though individual filaments shine only around magnitude 7.
