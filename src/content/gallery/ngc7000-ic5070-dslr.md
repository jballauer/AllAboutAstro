NGC 7000, the North America Nebula, and IC 5070, the Pelican Nebula, are a pair of large emission nebulae in Cygnus separated by a lane of obscuring dust, though they are part of the same enormous star-forming cloud. NGC 7000 lies roughly 2,590 light-years away, spans about 120 by 100 arcminutes, and shines near magnitude 4. IC 5070 lies at a similar distance, spans about 60 by 50 arcminutes, and shines near magnitude 8.
