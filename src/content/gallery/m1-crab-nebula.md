M1, the Crab Nebula, is the expanding wreckage of a supernova recorded by astronomers in the year 1054 AD, in the constellation Taurus. It lies about 6,500 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 8.4, and spans roughly 6 by 4 arcminutes. At its heart is the Crab Pulsar, a rapidly spinning neutron star left behind by the explosion. Approximate coordinates: RA 05h 34m 32s, Dec +22° 00′ 52″.
