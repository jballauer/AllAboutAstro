The total lunar eclipse of April 15, 2014, was the first of a "lunar tetrad" — four consecutive total lunar eclipses in 2014–2015. During totality the Moon passed deep into Earth's umbral shadow, reaching a maximum eclipse magnitude of about 1.29 and taking on a coppery-red "Blood Moon" color from sunlight refracted through Earth's atmosphere. The full event, from first to last partial contact, spanned roughly 3.5 hours.
