IC 405, the Flaming Star Nebula, and IC 410 are neighboring emission/reflection nebulae in the constellation Auriga, near the bright star Capella. IC 405 lies about 1,500 light-years away, shines around magnitude 6.0, and spans roughly 37 by 19 arcminutes. IC 410 lies farther, around 12,000 light-years away, shines near magnitude 7.5, and spans about 40 by 30 arcminutes; it hosts the open cluster NGC 1893 and the well-known "tadpole" globules.
