M31, the Andromeda Galaxy, is the nearest large spiral galaxy to the Milky Way, in the constellation Andromeda. It lies approximately 2.5 million light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of about 3.4 (making it the most distant object regularly visible to the naked eye), and spans an enormous 3°10′ by 1° across the sky — more than six times the width of the full Moon. Approximate coordinates: RA 00h 42m 44s, Dec +41° 16′.
