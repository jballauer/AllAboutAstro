The Flame Nebula (NGC 2024) and Horsehead Nebula (Barnard 33) sit side by side near the star Alnitak in Orion's belt. NGC 2024 is an emission nebula about 1,350 light-years away, spanning roughly 30 by 30 arcminutes. B33 is a dark dust cloud silhouetted against the glow of emission nebula IC 434 at a similar distance, spanning only about 6 by 4 arcminutes — small but famously distinctive in its horse-head silhouette.
