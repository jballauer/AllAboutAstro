NGC 5139, Omega Centauri, is the largest and brightest globular cluster orbiting the Milky Way, containing an estimated 10 million stars, in the constellation Centaurus. It lies about 17,090 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 3.7, and spans roughly 36 arcminutes — larger than the full Moon. From mid-northern latitudes it only skims the southern horizon. Approximate coordinates: RA 13h 26m 47s, Dec -47° 28′ 46″.
