The Helix Nebula (NGC 7293) is a planetary nebula — the glowing shell of gas cast off by a dying, Sun-like star — in the constellation Aquarius. It is one of the closest planetary nebulae to Earth, about 650 light-years away, which combined with its true size makes it appear unusually large: roughly 25 by 25 arcminutes, nearly the width of the full Moon, at an apparent magnitude of 7.6. Approximate coordinates: RA 22h 29m 38s, Dec -20° 50′.
