NGC 7000, the North America Nebula, is a large emission nebula in Cygnus whose shape resembles the North American continent; the "Cygnus Wall" is the bright, sharply-defined ridge of ionized gas and dust along its eastern edge where star formation is most active. The nebula lies roughly 2,590 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude near 4, and spans about 120 by 100 arcminutes. Approximate coordinates: RA 20h 59m 17s, Dec +44° 31′.
