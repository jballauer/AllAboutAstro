IC 1848, sometimes called the Embryo Nebula, is an emission nebula and open cluster forming the western half of the larger Soul Nebula (Westerhout 5) complex, in the constellation Cassiopeia. It lies roughly 7,500 light-years away, shines around magnitude 6.5, and spans about 60 by 30 arcminutes.
