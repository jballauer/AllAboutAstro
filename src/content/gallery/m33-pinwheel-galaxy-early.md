M33, often called the Pinwheel Galaxy (or the Triangulum Galaxy), is a face-on spiral and the third-largest member of the Local Group, in the small constellation Triangulum. It lies about 2.73 million light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 5.7, and spans roughly 70.8 by 41.7 arcminutes. Approximate coordinates: RA 01h 33m 50s, Dec +30° 39′.
