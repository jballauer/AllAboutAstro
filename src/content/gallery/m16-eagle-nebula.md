M16, the Eagle Nebula, is a young open cluster embedded in a large emission nebula, in the constellation Serpens. It contains the famous "Pillars of Creation," towering columns of gas and dust made iconic by the Hubble Space Telescope. It lies about 7,000 light-years away, shines at an apparent magnitude of 6.0, and spans roughly 70 by 50 arcminutes. Approximate coordinates: RA 18h 18m 48s, Dec -13° 49′.
