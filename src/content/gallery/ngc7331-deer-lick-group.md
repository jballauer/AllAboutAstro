NGC 7331 is a large, unbarred spiral galaxy in the constellation Pegasus, often called a near-twin of our own Milky Way. It lies about 40 million light-years away, shines at magnitude 9.5, and spans roughly 10.7 by 4.0 arcminutes. Nearby, a small chain of much more distant background galaxies (NGC 7335, 7336, 7337, 7338, and 7340) forms the "Deer Lick Group," a popular target for showing scale in deep-sky images.
