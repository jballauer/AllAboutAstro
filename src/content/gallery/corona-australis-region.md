The Corona Australis region centers on a compact but richly colorful complex of reflection and dark nebulae (including NGC 6726, NGC 6727, and NGC 6729) illuminated by young stars near R Coronae Australis. It is one of the closest star-forming regions to Earth, roughly 430 light-years away, in the small southern constellation Corona Australis, and spans several degrees of sky.
