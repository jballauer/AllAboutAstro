M8, the Lagoon Nebula, and M20, the Trifid Nebula, are neighboring star-forming emission/reflection nebulae in the constellation Sagittarius, both easily framed together in a wide field. M8 lies about 4,100 light-years away, shines at magnitude 6.0, and spans roughly 90 by 40 arcminutes. M20 lies farther, around 5,200 light-years away, shines at magnitude 6.3, and spans about 28 arcminutes, distinguished by the dark dust lanes that divide it into three lobes.
